# Client Space Analysis & Design Plan

## Space Analysis
- **Zone**: Rectangular fenced area ~6×8m, tree in center-back
- **Camera**: Elevated ~45° angle, wide angle, looking from front-left corner toward back-right
- **Fixed structures**: 
  - White picket fence (perimeter boundary — KEEP)
  - Large mature tree (center-back — KEEP EXACTLY)
  - Beige boundary wall (background)
  - Building wall (left)
  - Iron gate (far right)
  - Red-brown brick paving OUTSIDE fence
- **To remove/replace**: Wooden animal cages, dirt floor, debris inside fence
- **Challenge**: Tree is not perfectly centered — slightly right of center, near back wall

## 3 Design Directions

### DIRECTION A — "The Philosopher's Grove" (Symmetrical Imperial)
*Inspired by André Le Nôtre's formal order + Roberto Burle Marx color*
- Formal symmetrical layout using the tree as the supreme focal axis
- Octagonal paved floor of light/cream concrete tiles with dark border inlay
- 8 individual concrete chairs arranged in perfect octagon around the tree
- Concrete planter boxes at the fence perimeter, lush greenery
- Floor: geometric mosaic tiles (ref 174, 299)
- Chairs: concrete block seating (ref 130, 294)
- Tone: warm cream, charcoal, sand gold

### DIRECTION B — "The Sunken Council" (Sunken Circular Platform)
*Inspired by Peter Walker's minimalism + Thomas Church's human-scale*
- The interior ground is lowered ~2 steps to create a sunken conversation circle
- Circular/octagonal sunken platform with wide concrete step-seats doubling as perimeter benches
- 8 standalone concrete chairs on the sunken floor, tree emerges through center opening
- Concentric circle tile pattern radiating from tree base
- Elevated border rim becomes a planter/seating ledge
- Floor: circular radiating tiles (ref 289, 336)
- Chairs: sculptural concrete seats (ref 294, 87)
- Tone: charcoal dark concrete, warm sandstone

### DIRECTION C — "The Elevated Sanctuary" (Raised Platform Garden)
*Inspired by Ian McHarg's ecology + Piet Oudolf's naturalism*
- Raised platform ~2 steps up inside the fence, tree pierces through naturally
- 8 concrete chairs arranged in relaxed U/L-shape on the raised deck
- Raised stone-clad walls become seating ledges/planters at perimeter
- Lush planting pockets around tree base and along fence interior
- Floor: large format concrete tiles on raised deck
- Chairs: chunky concrete block chairs (ref 242, 336)
- Tone: warm sandstone, dark basalt border, lush green
