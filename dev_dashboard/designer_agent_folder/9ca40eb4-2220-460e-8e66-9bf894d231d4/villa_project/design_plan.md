## 3 DESIGN DIRECTIONS

### DIRECTION A — "Imperial Andalusian Court"
*Philosophy: André Le Nôtre axial symmetry + Islamic garden tradition (charbagh)*
- **Concept**: Grand formal symmetrical forecourt channeling the Alhambra and Versailles simultaneously. Strict bilateral symmetry along entrance axis.
- **Layout**: 
  - Grand geometric paving medallion at center — star/octagon artisan floor pattern in warm cream/beige/terracotta tones from Ducon catalog
  - Central grand tiered cascade fountain on medallion (from catalog id 54 style)
  - Twin parallel raised planter beds flanking axis (from catalog id 205 tiered blocks with waterfall element)
  - Entry walkway paved in rich warm stone tiles (catalog 54 pattern)
  - Twin majestic date palms symmetrically placed left/right of medallion
  - Flowering border plantings in planter beds (bougainvillea pinks, jasmine whites)
  - Secondary paving panels with diamond/cross pattern beside planters
- **Materials**: Warm sandstone/cream large format tiles, darker accent border bands, ornate Ducon artisan medallion floor insert
- **Variations**:
  - A1: Gold/warm tones with dramatic tiered fountain centerpiece
  - A2: White/ivory with reflecting pool channel instead of fountain
  - A3: Multi-level with raised viewing platform flanked by pillar elements

### DIRECTION B — "Contemporary Gulf Prestige"  
*Philosophy: Peter Walker minimalist geometry + Piet Oudolf naturalist planting*
- **Concept**: Modern luxury — bold, clean geometry meets lush organic planting. Ducon modern floor tiles in large format contrasting warm and cool.
- **Layout**:
  - Sleek large-format floor tiles in warm beige + dark grey/charcoal accent strips
  - Offset geometric planter boxes (Ducon raised planters) left and right — planted with tall ornamental grasses, purple lavender, flowering masses
  - Elegant narrow rill water feature running from center toward entrance (linear water channel)
  - Twin pergolas on left and right mid-zone framing the approach (catalog 122/262 style)
  - BBQ/outdoor kitchen on left side near annex (catalog 292 style)
  - Seating pod under left pergola
  - Modern bollard lighting
- **Materials**: Warm grey large slabs, dark charcoal linear accents, white/cream planter boxes
- **Variations**:
  - B1: Dual flanking pergolas with seating + rill water feature
  - B2: Single grand pergola canopy on one side + pool on other
  - B3: Bold contrast black/white/warm paving with spiral planter compositions

### DIRECTION C — "Desert Oasis Magnificence"
*Philosophy: Roberto Burle Marx organic curved forms + Thomas Church California style*
- **Concept**: Organic flowing forms echoing the curved rotunda of the villa. Sweeping curves in paving, lush oasis planting. Feels abundant, alive, magical.
- **Layout**:
  - Organic curved paving pattern (arcing bands) from camera to entrance — cream base with dark grey wave/ribbon accents (catalog 16 radial approach echoed)
  - Large central oval/circular elevated garden island with specimen trees (Flame tree / Royal Poinciana)
  - Curved raised planter walls on left and right — textured stone blocks (catalog 205)
  - Curved pool element on far left — freeform shaped pool with palm deck
  - Seating area under pergola on right with outdoor furniture
  - Dense tropical planting including date palms, fan palms, bougainvillea climbers
  - Flowing grass lawns between curved paving bands
- **Materials**: Curved artisan floor tiles, natural stone planter blocks, timber pergola
- **Variations**:
  - C1: Lush sweeping oasis with oval garden island + flanking water features
  - C2: Curved pool as dominant feature on left with oasis planting
  - C3: Raised garden platform over curved stairs + cascading planting terraces
